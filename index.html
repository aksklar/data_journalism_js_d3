Poverty and Tobacco Use

In the scatter plot chart above, we are investigating the relationships between demographic information and health risk (poverty vs. tobacco use in particular). The data collected was from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System for the year of 2014. Each point in the scatter represents a state (hover over point for more information). The scatter plot shows a positive correlation between rate of poverty and the use of tobacco. The trend shows that as rate of poverty increases, so does the rate of tobacco use. Some of the southern states lead in the trend and that can be explained by the affordability and acceptance of tobacco products whereas states like New York and California have stricter laws of the use of tobacco and the heavy taxes on tobacco products. The outlier in this scatter plot would be Utah. States with the similar rate of poverty to Utah would have a 14% to 16% rate of tobacco use, whereas Utah's tobacco use is the lowest of all 50 states (9.7%). This can be explained by the huge Mormon population in Utah.
